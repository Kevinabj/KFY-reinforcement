    % ---------- Setup and taxonomy ----------
    \begin{block}{2. Setup and taxonomy}
        We re-implement four canonical deep RL algorithms from scratch in PyTorch (no \texttt{stable-baselines3}) and benchmark them with 3 seeds each, 95\% bootstrap CIs over seeds, and four pre-registered ablations.

        \vspace{0.3em}
        \begin{center}
        \small
        \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.2}
        \begin{tabular}{@{}l|cc@{}}
            \toprule
                                  & \textbf{on-policy} & \textbf{off-policy} \\
            \midrule
            \textbf{value-based}  & (none)            & \algotag[dqncol]{DQN} \\
            \textbf{policy-based} & \algotag[ppocol]{PPO} & \algotag[saccol]{SAC}, \algotag[td3col]{TD3} \\
            \bottomrule
        \end{tabular}
        \end{center}

        \textbf{Networks.} $(64,64)$ MLPs for DQN, PPO; $(256,256)$ for SAC, TD3 (canonical paper widths). \quad \textbf{Hardware.} RTX 3070 + EPFL gnoto V100.
    \end{block}

    % ---------- Algorithms ----------
    \begin{block}{3. Algorithms}
        \small
        \algocard{dqncol}{DQN \cite{mnih2013playing}}{%
          value-based, off-policy, discrete:
          \[
          \mathcal{L} = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\ell_{\text{Hub}}\!\big(Q_\theta - r - \gamma(1{-}d)\textstyle\max_{a'}Q_{\bar{\theta}}\big)\right]
          \]
          $\varepsilon$-greedy, target sync every $\tau$ steps, Huber loss.}\hfill
        \algocard{ppocol}{PPO \cite{schulman2017proximal}}{%
          policy-based, on-policy:
          \[
          \mathcal{L} = -\mathbb{E}\!\left[\min\!\big(r_t\hat{A}_t,\, \mathrm{clip}(r_t, 1{\pm}\epsilon)\hat{A}_t\big)\right]
          \]
          GAE advantages, clipped surrogate as a soft trust region.}

        \vspace{0.6em}

        \algocard{saccol}{SAC \cite{haarnoja2018soft}}{%
          off-policy, max-entropy:
          \[
          J = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\textstyle\sum_t \gamma^t\big(r_t + \alpha\,\mathcal{H}[\pi(\cdot|s_t)]\big)\right]
          \]
          Squashed-Gaussian actor, auto $\alpha$ via $\log\alpha$, twin Q.}\hfill
        \algocard{td3col}{TD3 \cite{fujimoto2018addressing}}{%
          off-policy, deterministic:
          \[
          y = r + \gamma(1{-}d)\textstyle\min_{i}Q_{\bar{\phi}_i}\!\big(s', \pi_{\bar{\theta}}{+}\tilde{\epsilon}\big)
          \]
          Twin critics, target smoothing $\tilde{\sigma}{=}0.2$, delayed actor.}
    \end{block}

    % ---------- Implementation details ----------
    \begin{block}{4. Implementation details that mattered}
        \small
        \begin{itemize}
            \itemsep0.15em
            \item \algotag[dqncol]{DQN}: target-sync sweep on CartPole gives a \hl{U-shape} ($\tau\in\{100, 1000, 5000\}$). The canonical $\tau{=}1000$ sits at the edge of stability, which explains the seed-bimodality.
            \item \algotag[ppocol]{PPO}: at Pendulum truncation we fold $\gamma V(s_{t{+}1})$ into the reward so GAE accounts for the value of the truncated state; raw \texttt{done} flags bias returns by $\sim$30.
            \item \algotag[saccol]{SAC}: the tanh-Jacobian log-prob correction $-\sum_i \log(1-\tanh^2 u_i)$ is mandatory; without it the entropy term is silently wrong and training never moves off the warmup floor.
            \item \algotag[td3col]{TD3}: the smoothed target action needs \emph{two} clips, on the noise ($\pm 0.5$) and on the action range. Skipping the outer clip sticks the actor on the boundary.
            \item \textbf{All four}: bootstrap mask uses \texttt{terminated} only, not \texttt{terminated\,or\,truncated}.
        \end{itemize}
    \end{block}

        \vspace{0.5em}
        \small
        \textbf{Why off-policy continuous methods fail on MCC.} Entropy regularization (SAC) and additive Gaussian noise (TD3) drive uniform coverage of the \emph{local} action set, not directed exploration in state space. PPO's on-policy rollouts occasionally produce a long correlated action sequence that crosses the hill; off-policy replay samples are decorrelated and cannot.
    \end{alertblock}

    % ---------- Limitations and open questions ----------
    \begin{block}{6. Limitations \& open questions}
        \small
        \begin{itemize}
            \itemsep0.1em
            \item \textbf{Seed count.} 3 seeds per (algo, env). More would tighten CIs but cost is super-linear in our compute budget; the 3-seed bootstrap CIs are wide enough to keep our claims honest.
            \item \textbf{Network widths fixed.} $(64,64)$ for DQN, PPO and $(256,256)$ for SAC, TD3 (canonical paper defaults). The actor-parameter gap is a hyperparameter choice, not an algorithmic property, and we say so when comparing model sizes.
            \item \textbf{Sparse reward $=$ MCC only.} Generalizing the headline (off-policy exploration gap on sparse reward) needs a second sparse-reward continuous benchmark, for example \texttt{Reacher} with a binary success reward or a sparsified \texttt{Pendulum}.
            \item \textbf{No exploration-baseline comparison.} Natural next step: RND or NoisyNet on top of SAC/TD3 and re-run the MCC ablation. Does directed exploration close the gap that local entropy/noise cannot?
        \end{itemize}
    \end{block}

    % ---------- Decision diagram + practical rule ----------
    \begin{exampleblock}{C. When to use which algorithm}
        \centering
        \decisionTree

        \vspace{0.6em}
        \begin{itemize}
            \itemsep0.1em
            \small
            \item \textbf{Discrete actions}: \algotag[dqncol]{DQN} wins on Acrobot, bimodal on CartPole; \algotag[ppocol]{PPO} is more reliable.
            \item \textbf{Dense-reward continuous}: \algotag[saccol]{SAC} and \algotag[td3col]{TD3} dominate, $12.9\times$ over \algotag[ppocol]{PPO} on Pendulum.
            \item \textbf{Sparse-reward continuous}: \algotag[ppocol]{PPO} is the only one to find the MCC goal; the failure is structural, not a tuning issue.
        \end{itemize}
        \centering
        \textbf{\large Pick SAC or TD3 first on continuous control; switch to PPO (or add explicit exploration bonuses) the moment reward becomes sparse.}
    \end{exampleblock}
